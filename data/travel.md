# 2026 FIFA World Cup Travel Guide

## Getting Around
The 2026 World Cup spans three countries and 16 cities. The schedule is designed to minimize travel, with matches clustered into Western, Central, and Eastern regions.

## Regions
- Western Region: Vancouver, Seattle, Los Angeles, San Francisco Bay Area, Guadalajara
- Central Region: Mexico City, Monterrey, Houston, Dallas, Kansas City
- Eastern Region: Atlanta, Miami, Philadelphia, New York/New Jersey, Boston, Toronto

## Key Travel Tips
- Matches within groups are clustered by region to reduce fan travel
- Host nations (USA, Mexico, Canada) play all three group games at home venues
- Three days rest between most matches allows travel time for fans following teams
- Major hub airports: LAX, DFW, JFK/EWR, ATL, MIA, YYZ

## Broadcast Information
- All 104 matches available on Peacock (English and Spanish)
- Telemundo broadcasts 92 matches
- Universo carries 12 matches
- Global broadcast partners in all FIFA member nations

## Time Zones
- Matches scheduled to accommodate global audiences
- Kick-off times range from 12:00 to 22:00 local time
- Eastern Time (ET) is the primary reference for US scheduling
