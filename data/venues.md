# 2026 FIFA World Cup Venues

## United States (11 venues)
- Atlanta: Mercedes-Benz Stadium (8 matches, semi-final host)
- Boston: Gillette Stadium (7 matches)
- Dallas: AT&T Stadium (9 matches - most of any city, semi-final host)
- Houston: NRG Stadium (7 matches)
- Kansas City: Arrowhead Stadium (6 matches)
- Los Angeles: SoFi Stadium (8 matches, USA opener)
- Miami: Hard Rock Stadium (7 matches, third-place match)
- New York/New Jersey: MetLife Stadium (8 matches, THE FINAL)
- Philadelphia: Lincoln Financial Field (6 matches)
- San Francisco Bay Area: Levi's Stadium (6 matches)
- Seattle: Lumen Field (6 matches)

## Mexico (3 venues)
- Mexico City: Mexico City Stadium (5 matches, tournament opener)
- Guadalajara: Estadio Akron (4 matches)
- Monterrey: Estadio BBVA (4 matches)

## Canada (2 venues)
- Toronto: BMO Field (6 matches, Canada opener)
- Vancouver: BC Place (7 matches)

## Venue Facts
- First World Cup hosted across three countries
- Dallas hosts the most matches (9) of any single city
- Mexico City Stadium (formerly Estadio Azteca) hosts an opening match for the third time in World Cup history
- MetLife Stadium capacity: approximately 82,500 for the final
- All venues feature climate considerations for summer matches
